Core Foundations

Supreme law establishing a Federal Parliamentary Republic. Balances federal and provincial powers.

Ceremonial Head of State. Represents national unity, elected by electoral college for 5 years.
⚖️
Prime Minister
Chief Executive & Head of Government. Must command majority in the National Assembly.
🤝
Federal Cabinet
Assists the PM in executive functions. Collectively responsible to the National Assembly.
Section 02

National Assembly — Lower House

Role & Authority

  • Directly elected body representing the population
  • Fixed 5-year term unless dissolved earlier
  • Exclusive authority over Money Bills (budget & tax)
  • Prime Minister elected from among its members
  • Can remove PM via Vote of No-Confidence

Key Power

Only the National Assembly can originate and pass Money Bills — giving it supreme control over national finances.

Section 02

The Senate — Upper House

Purpose & Structure

  • Ensures equal provincial representation
  • Permanent body — cannot be dissolved
  • Members serve staggered 6-year terms
  • 96 seats total (post-FATA merger)
  • Debates all federal legislation except Money Bills

Why It Matters

The Senate prevents the larger provinces from dominating legislation, acting as a guardian of federalism.

Section 03

How Laws Are Made

1
Introduction (1st Reading)Bill introduced in either House (Money Bills only in NA). Title read aloud.
2
Debate (2nd Reading)General principles discussed. Members debate the need and scope of the Bill.
3
Committee StageReferred to the relevant Standing Committee for clause-by-clause scrutiny and amendments.
4
Final Vote (3rd Reading)Voted on in the originating House, then transmitted to the other House for the same process.
5
Presidential Assent → LawSigned by the President. Deadlocks resolved via Joint Sitting of both Houses.
Section 03

Accountability & Oversight

🔍 Standing Committees

Monitor ministry performance, scrutinize budgets, and recommend corrective action.

❓ Question Hour

Members hold ministers accountable by asking direct questions about their ministry's activities during every session.

📋 Public Accounts Committee

Reviews all government spending to ensure funds are used lawfully and efficiently.

⚡ Vote of No-Confidence

Any PM who loses the confidence of the majority in the National Assembly must resign. First successfully used in 2022.

🚨 Adjournment Motions

Opposition can force debate on urgent public issues — keeping the government answerable in real time.

Committees are the eyes and ears of Parliament.

Section 04

Systemic Challenges

📃

Presidential Ordinances

  • Issued when Parliament is not in session
  • Valid for only 120 days
  • Routine re-promulgation bypasses democratic debate
💰

Senate & Finance Gap

  • Senate has advisory powers only on Money Bills
  • Cannot veto National Assembly financial legislation
  • Weakens provincial say in federal budget
⚡

Political Instability

  • Fragile coalitions cause policy inconsistency
  • Opposition boycotts paralyze legislation
  • Deep polarization blocks national consensus

Section 05

Electoral Reform Agenda

🖥️

Electronic Voting Machines

Ongoing national debate on adopting EVMs. Supporters cite speed and fraud reduction; critics question reliability and transparency.

🗺️

Delimitation of Constituencies

Redrawing of constituency boundaries remains contested, with concerns over gerrymandering and population accuracy.

✈️

Overseas Voting Rights

Over 9 million Pakistanis abroad. Granting them voting rights could significantly shift electoral outcomes.

🎯

Core Goal

Modernize the election system to ensure credible, transparent, and inclusive elections — the bedrock of parliamentary legitimacy.

Section 06

Recommendations

⬆️

Empower the Senate

Grant the Upper House greater authority on Money Bills to ensure meaningful provincial input on national finances.

🔒

Limit Ordinances

Enforce strict constitutional limits on re-promulgation to restore legislative supremacy of Parliament.

🔬

Strengthen Committees

Provide independent research staff, expert witnesses, and broader investigative powers to parliamentary committees.

Section 06

Conclusion

🛡️ Constitutional Resilience

Despite martial laws, political crises, and institutional breakdowns, Pakistan's parliamentary framework has endured and evolved since 1973.

🔑 Path Forward

  • Electoral integrity must be non-negotiable
  • Separation of powers must be strictly upheld
  • Institutional maturity over short-term gains
🔭

Outlook 2026

Pakistan stands at a democratic crossroads. Navigating electoral reform, economic stability, and civil–military balance will define the next chapter of parliamentary governance.

Democracy is not a destination — it is a continuous journey of accountability and reform.
